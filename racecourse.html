Reading the Course

Start: cross the line between the committee boat and the pin, sailing upwind. Windward mark: leave it to port, then the short hop to the small yellow offset mark keeps boats clear of traffic still beating up. Leeward gate: two orange tetrahedrons — pick either one, round it the natural way, and head back upwind. Choosing the gate side that takes you toward the favored side of the next beat is free distance. Finish: between the committee boat and the green mark off its starboard side.

Triangle (Olympic): beat to the windward mark, bear away onto the reach to the jibe mark, jibe, reach back to the leeward mark, and beat again. Reaches reward clear air — defend high early, then drive low for speed.

Race Scoring

CLSA series are scored with the Low Point System (Racing Rules of Sailing, Appendix A): your finishing place is your score — 1st = 1 point, 2nd = 2, and so on — and the lowest total wins the series. A boat that doesn't get a clean finish is scored as if she finished one place behind every boat entered in the series. With enough races sailed, each boat throws out her worst score (or two), so one bad day doesn't sink a season.

Ties: if two boats end level on points, line up each boat's scores from best to worst and compare — first boat with a better score wins the tie. Still level? The boat that beat the other in the last race takes it.
